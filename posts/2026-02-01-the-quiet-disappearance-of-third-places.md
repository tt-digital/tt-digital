Ray Oldenburg called them "third places": spaces beyond home and work where people can simply exist. Pubs, barbershops, bookstores, coffeehouses.

They're getting rarer. Rents rise, spots close, dwell time shrinks. People order online, wear headphones, sit side by side without looking up.

> A place worth staying in needs no agenda. Just chairs and time.

## What Gets Lost

When third places vanish, we lose the accidental encounter — conversations that were never planned but somehow changed something.
