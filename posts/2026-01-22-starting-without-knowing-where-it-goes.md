---
title: Starting Without Knowing Where It Goes
date: 2026-01-22
tag: thought
excerpt: Most good things begin with uncertainty. That's not a bug — it's the feature.
---

I rarely know how a piece ends when I begin it. I have a sentence, a mood, maybe an image. And then I write through it — searching, stumbling, sometimes lost.

That's not a bad sign. It's the only thing that separates real writing from templates.

The idea that you need to know everything before you start is one of the most paralyzing lies of productivity culture. Clarity often comes only in the doing.
