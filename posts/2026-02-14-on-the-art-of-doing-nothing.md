title: On the Art of Doing Nothing
date: 2026-02-14

excerpt: In a world that worships productivity, idleness is almost an act of resistance.
---

There are afternoons when I do nothing but stare out the window. No book, no podcast, no list to check off. Just the sky and the slow settling of thought, like dust after a long day.

We've forgotten how to be bored. Yet boredom is the antechamber of creativity — the place where ideas surface because there's finally room for them.

> "Idleness is the root of all evil" — perhaps. But it's also the mother of every good idea.

## What Silence Teaches

I've started carving out stretches where nothing happens. Not meditation — that's just another task. Real nothing. And inside it, there's a strange kind of freedom.
