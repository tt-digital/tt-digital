We talk a lot about beginnings. First drafts, new projects, fresh starts. The mythology of the blank page. What we talk about less is the specific satisfaction of finishing something — really finishing it, not abandoning it at ninety percent.

Finishing requires a different kind of discipline than starting. Starting runs on excitement. Finishing runs on commitment to something that no longer feels new.

> Done is not a compromise. Done is the whole point.

## The Ninety Percent Problem

Most things die in the last stretch. The book that's almost written. The project that just needs one more pass. The letter that's been sitting in drafts for three weeks.

I've started treating the final ten percent as a separate task with its own energy budget. Not a continuation of the work — a completion of it. The difference in framing matters more than it should.
