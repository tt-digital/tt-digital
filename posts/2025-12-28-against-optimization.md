There is a certain kind of person who, confronted with any process, immediately asks how it could be done faster. I used to be that person. I optimized my morning, my reading, my routes to work. I got very good at doing things quickly and lost any sense of why I was doing them.

Optimization is seductive because it feels like progress. You're improving. You're getting better. But better at what, exactly?

> Efficiency is a good servant and a terrible master.

## The Cost of Frictionlessness

Friction slows you down. It also makes you pay attention. The long way round a city teaches you the city. The slow read of a difficult book teaches you to think. Remove the friction and you remove the learning that lives inside it.

Some of the best conversations I've had started because something went wrong and we had to wait. Some of the best ideas arrived in the space that inefficiency created.
